John Conway's Game of Life is a mathematical demonstration showing how, based on initial conditions, cells can live or die with each passing generation. 

The rules are as follows:
1. Cells with less than two live neighors die.
2. Cells with more than three live neighbors die.
3. Cells with two or three live neghbors survive to the next generation but are unchanged.
4. Dead cells with three live neighbors regenerate.
