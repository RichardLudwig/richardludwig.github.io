I find there are four requirements to learn a language

They are as follows:
1. To read
2. To write (or type)
3. To listen
4. To speak

If you can do all four in a language, you can communicate. Communication does not have to be perfect to be effective. My personal goal is simple. I learn languages to be a more effective traveler. I'm not attempting to translate Shakespeare or give a speech to the UN. If I can read signage and ask for directions, I'm content. Your goals may differ, requiring more intense preparation. 

The online materials I use are as follows, and in no order of importance:
- Duolingo (reading, writing, listening)
- FSI (listening, speaking)
- Pimsleur (listening, speaking)
- Clozemaster (reading, writing, listening)
- Drops (reading, writing, listening)

I use these materials in combination, depending on the language being learned. While other materials may be used, these are the ones I use most consistently.
