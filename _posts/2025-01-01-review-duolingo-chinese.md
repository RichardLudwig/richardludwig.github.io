I completed the Duolingo Chinese course, and I have some thoughts...

- Avoid using Pinyin hints and subtitles, so you can associate logographic characters and radicals with concepts and words, and not Romanized transliterations. 
- Consider muting the website to temporarily limit the difficulty of the course. Associating characters and radicals with their English translations, as well as the sounds of the Mandarin language, may result in information overload, which can hamper learning and motivation. You can always unmute the site once you feel comfortable with the course learning materials.
- Specialize on the skills you want to learn. I completed the course because I wanted to learn to read basic Mandarin, and Duolingo is a good resource for that. If I wanted to learn to speak Mandarin, I would use a resource more attuned to that goal, like <a target="_blank" href="https://www.italki.com/">Italki</a>, an online language tutoring platform.  
- If you make a mistake, spend some time to learn why you made the mistake. Use Google to understand why you made the error before moving to a more advanced level.
